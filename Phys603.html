Phys603 — Graduate Math Methods in Physics


Purpose of this course

To develop your ‘toolbox’ of mathematical techniques, ready to apply to the problems you’ll encounter in physics research.

Books

There is no single recommended book for this class. Here are some that i'm familar with:

  • Byron & Fuller Mathematics of Classical and Quantum Physics. A cheap Dover reprint containing all the material we'll cover.
  • Mary L. Boas Mathematical Methods in the Physical Sciences. The best undergraduate level book. Currently in third edition, older editions are just as good.
  • Riley, Hobson & Bense Mathematical Methods for Physics and Engineering. Technically an undergraduate level book, but long and rather complete.
  • Arfken et al. Mathematical Methods for Physicists. Used at both the undergraduate and graduate levels.

Topics

  • Complex analysis
  • Linear vector spaces
  • Integral transforms
  • Green's functions
  • Introductory group theory
